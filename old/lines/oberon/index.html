Oberon Branch

  • History
  • Maps

The Oberon Branch is a short line in the mountains to the west of Sydney. It extends south from the Main West Line to the town of Oberon. Open for 56 years, it was closed in 1979.

Leaving the Main West at Tarana, the line meandered south to Carlwood where it began the 10km climb to Hazelgrove. This section featured some of the steepest (1 in 25 compensated) and tightest (5 chain radius) curves in the state. From there, the line proceeded toward the terminus of Oberon.

There were some suggestions in recent years that it would be re-opened to service CSR in Oberon. However, it was announced in late 2000 that an agreement has been reached between Oberon Council and the Rail Access Corporation, to turn the line into a walking track.
