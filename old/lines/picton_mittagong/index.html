Picton Loop Line

  • History

The Picton-Mittagong Loop Line was part of the original Main South Line. Originally built in 1867, it was replaced by a new deviation in 1919.

Today the central section is closed, with the section between Buxton and Picton being used by the Railway Transport Museum for tourist operations. The RTM runs weekend and other occasional tours from its base at Thirlmere.

The southern end is still in use, but only to service a concrete sleeper supplier and Clyde engineering, at Braemar. The line is cut near Braemar by a missing railway bridge.
