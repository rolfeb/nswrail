Research

This page contains some some articles and resources which may prove useful to people interested in learning more about NSW railways.
ARHS(NSW) Resource Centre
The Australian Railway Historic Society (NSW) Resource Centre is a large repository of materials covering the history of railways in NSW. They are open to the public.

NSW Track Diagrams and Circulars
If you are interested in Track Diagrams and Circulars for NSW, then the "NSW Railway Track and Signal Diagrams" CD from the ARHS - NSW division is highly recommended.

Names of Railway Stations in New South Wales
Patrick James has kindly scanned in C. A. Irish's 1927 text, Names of Railway Stations in New South Wales. With their Meaning and Origin.. The paper is available online. A more up-to-date reference is John Forsyth's How And Why of Station Names: Meanings and Origins, which you may find in a reasonable-sized library.

ARHS Bulletin / Australian Railway History index
The ARHS Bulletin magazine is a magazine which caters to people interested in Australian Railway history. It has been in print for over 60 years and is an invaluable source of historical information. It was recently renamed Australian Railway History. Howard Quinlan created an index of the Bulletin for the years up to 1987. This was put into electronic form and made available by David Virgo. Finally, Geoff Lambert made corrections and added entries up to Dec 2000 (now updated to Feb 2002 by me).

I have created a search page allows you to find articles by various criteria. The articles themselves are not online, but you could check in a major library or visit the ARHS Railway Resource Centre to read them.

Historic Parish Maps
The NSW Department of Lands has made a huge range of old parish maps available. If you are trying to track down old branches or stations in NSW, these maps are invaluable. You can find out which parish a location belongs to at the NSW Geographic Names Board's register search.

South Maitland Collieries
I have written a short summary article on the collieries of the South Maitland Railway.

Widemere Quarry Branch
Karina Hall has written an article on this branch which ran from Fairfield to the nearby quarry.

Safeworking Documents
The NSW RIC (Railway Infrastructure Corporation) has made available the Safeworking Online page, covering the rules and regulations for operations in NSW.
